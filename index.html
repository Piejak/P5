Breaking down the Blockbusters - An exploration of the data behind the decade's highest grossing movies
By Brian Piejak
If we look at all of the movies in the last decade that have grossed over $100,000,000, what similarities can we find in the data? Is there a formula the movie studios are using to churn out their biggest moneymakers? Let's find out.
People love superheroes
and Twilight is their guilty pleasure
Of the list of movies grossing more than $100,000,000 colored according to their rating on IMDB, the top grossing are primarily superhero movies all with high ratings. The Twilight saga are the largest circles with the worst ratings. People don't like the movies, but something keeps them coming back to the theaters.
You don't have to spend money to make money
Adjusting for the profit each movie made by subtracting the budget from gross earnings we see a lot more parity in the sizes of the movies in the graph. We also see the lower rated movies growing in size, suggesting that the studios might be sacrificing quality for a lower budget to turn more of a profit and consumers are picking up on this in the form of ratings.
Just explicit enough
The majority of the movies as well as those making the most profit are rated PG-13. Some R rated films made comparable profits, but it seems like a studio exec's safest bet is to make sure teens can get into the theater without a parent.
Length doesn't matter
It seems that consumers aren't necessarily concerned about getting the most bang for their buck in terms of movie length. With the trend of movies becoming longer, you might expect to see the lengthier flicks turning more of a profit. However, films like The Avenger and Iron Man 3, both around three hours long have comparable profits to Jurrasic World which has a full hour less of runtime. The darkest dot on the plot - The Wolf of Wall Street, clocking in at a whopping four hours long, is relatively small in terms of profit made.
